## Foundational mental models

### The Value Equation — the only equation that matters
> **Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)**
> *Source: [100m-offers.md](hormozi/100m-offers.md) — "Value Offer: The Value Equation"*

- **Two levers UP:** Dream Outcome, Perceived Likelihood.
- **Two levers DOWN:** Time Delay, Effort & Sacrifice.
- Every offer, ad, landing page, sales script, and product decision should move one of these four levers.
- When something isn't selling, ask: which lever is weakest? Fix that one first.

### Charge what it's worth — the Virtuous Cycle of Price
> *Source: [100m-offers.md](hormozi/100m-offers.md) — "Pricing: Charge What It's Worth" + [playbook-price-raise.md](hormozi/playbook-price-raise.md)*

Premium pricing → higher client emotional investment → higher perceived value → better results → more profit → better service → wider moat. Discounting does the reverse and starts a death spiral. **"There is no strategic benefit to being the second-lowest priced player in your marketplace."**

For Hughie: don't anchor on what trades-services usually pay for software. Anchor on the dollar value of leads recovered. Speed-to-lead saves a $2K-$10K job per missed call. Price accordingly.

### Volume negates luck
> *Source: [playbook-lead-nurture.md](hormozi/playbook-lead-nurture.md) — Pillar IV*
> *Source: [100m-leads.md](hormozi/100m-leads.md) — "Rule of 100"*

Most communication attempts fail. Half of salespeople quit after attempt #1. Volume is the highest-EV intervention before optimization. Before tweaking copy, **double the outputs**.

### Open To Goal
> *Source: [100m-leads.md](hormozi/100m-leads.md) — "Advertising in Real Life: Open To Goal"*

Don't work to a clock. Work to an outcome. "Sign 5 new members today" — if it takes till lunch, leave early. If it takes 18 hours, stay 18 hours. This unlocks a level of effort that time-boxed work never reaches.

### Rule of 100
> *Source: [100m-leads.md](hormozi/100m-leads.md) — "Core Four On Steroids: More Better New"*

100 productive Core-Four actions per day. Posts, DMs, calls, ads-launched — whatever moves the lead-gen needle. For Hughie pre-revenue: 100 prospect touches/day on electricians.

## OFFERS — make it so good they feel stupid saying no

### Grand Slam Offer (GSO)
> *Source: [100m-offers.md](hormozi/100m-offers.md) — "Grand Slam Offers"*

Definition: an offer presented so well that buyers feel **stupid saying no**. Built by maximizing all four Value Equation levers, then enhancing with Scarcity, Urgency, Bonuses, Guarantees, and Naming.

Hughie's speed-to-lead GSO frame:
- **Dream outcome:** never miss another job. Every after-hours call becomes a booked appointment.
- **Likelihood:** "We've recovered $X for [Wollongong electrician]. Here's the dashboard."
- **Time delay:** "Live in 48 hours."
- **Effort:** "We install it. You do nothing."

### Enhancing the Offer: SUBGN
> *Source: [100m-offers.md](hormozi/100m-offers.md) — "Scarcity, Urgency, Bonuses, Guarantees, and Naming"*

- **Scarcity:** real cap on supply. "I take 3 electrician clients per quarter."
- **Urgency:** real deadline. "Onboarding closes Friday."
- **Bonuses:** stack things that solve adjacent pains. Each bonus gets a price tag, a why, and a value.
- **Guarantees:** reverse risk. Conditional ("if X doesn't happen, we Y"), unconditional ("money back"), anti-guarantee ("you keep the system either way"). A bold guarantee is itself a sales tool.
- **Naming:** the offer needs a name people can repeat. "The Missed-Call Money Machine" > "speed-to-lead automation."

### Solving Problems (Offer Construction)
> *Source: [100m-offers.md](hormozi/100m-offers.md) — "Value Offer: Creating Your Grand Slam Offer Part I"*

1. List every reason a prospect *won't* succeed (using the four value drivers).
2. Turn each problem into a solution embedded in the offer.
3. Each solution becomes a deliverable or bonus.

## LEADS — getting strangers to want to buy

### Core Four — the only 4 ways to advertise
> *Source: [100m-leads.md](hormozi/100m-leads.md) — "Core Four"*

|  | People who know you | People who don't |
|---|---|---|
| **1-to-1 (private)** | Warm Outreach | Cold Outreach |
| **1-to-∞ (public)** | Post Free Content | Run Paid Ads |

Every lead source compresses into one of these four. Master all four over time. Start with the one with the shortest distance to first dollar — for Hughie, that's **Warm Outreach** (electricians he or his network already know) → **Cold Outreach** (Wollongong trades list) → **Content** (case studies) → **Paid Ads** (after first 2 case studies).

### More, Better, New
> *Source: [100m-leads.md](hormozi/100m-leads.md) — "Core Four On Steroids"*

Order of leverage:
1. **More** — double the input. Biggest near-term gain. Do this first.
2. **Better** — split-test and improve conversion. Do this after More plateaus.
3. **New** — add a new core-four activity, new platform, new placement.

### Lead Magnets — 7 steps
> *Source: [100m-leads.md](hormozi/100m-leads.md) — "Engage Your Leads: Offers and Lead Magnets"*

1. Pick the problem + who you solve it for.
2. Figure out how to solve it.
3. Figure out how to deliver it (PDF, tool, audit, sample).
4. Test the name.
5. Make it easy to consume.
6. Make it *darn good* — "be the drug dealer, give the strongest hit first."
7. Make it easy for them to say "I want more."

Lead magnet types include: free sample, one step of a multi-step process, software/tool, free audit/diagnostic, free strategy call.

For Hughie's electricians: **"Free Missed-Call Audit"** — pull their phone log + Google Business reviews, hand them a one-pager showing dollars left on the table this month.

### Hooks — what gets the first 2 seconds
> *Source: [playbook-hooks.md](hormozi/playbook-hooks.md) — "Hooks That Get Clicks"*

A hook is whatever gets attention. Categories:
- **Questions:** "If you're working all the time and your business isn't growing, you're working on the wrong sh*t."
- **Commands:** "Read this if you're tired of being broke."
- **Statements:** "The smartest thing you can do today…"
- **Lists/Steps:** "28 ways to stay poor."
- **Narratives:** "One day this old lady came in piss-angry…"
- **Exclamations:** "Ahhhhh… here's the blueprint."

Hooks can be visual or auditory, not just words. Specific enough to attract the right people, broad enough to attract *enough* of them.

### GOATed Ads
> *Source: [playbook-goated-ads.md](hormozi/playbook-goated-ads.md)*

The playbook structure: (1) why most ads hit a ceiling, (2) the ad creation process, (3) types of ads = endless creative buckets, (4) the **ad kaleidoscope** — recombine ad atoms into infinite variations. Pull this file when running paid for the first time.

### Lead Nurture — three pillars
> *Source: [playbook-lead-nurture.md](hormozi/playbook-lead-nurture.md)*

- **Pillar I: Speed.** Reach leads in <5 minutes. This is what speed-to-lead *IS*.
- **Pillar II: Personalization.** Match testimonials to the lead's demographics; reduce risk + increase cost-of-missing.
- **Pillar III: Send best leads to best closers.**
- **Pillar IV: Volume.** Reach out again. Most attempts fail for boring reasons (timing, forgot, missed). ~Half of salespeople quit after attempt 1.

## CLOSING — turning yeses into payments

### General principles
> *Source: [playbook-closing.md](hormozi/playbook-closing.md)*

1. **Stack closes.** If one works, try a second. Different angles for the same objection.
2. **Understand the logic** — don't memorize scripts. Logic lets you improvise in your own words.
3. **2–3 closes per main obstacle is enough.** Don't try to learn 100.
4. **Yellows are golds.** Practice on unqualified leads. Volume of reps = skill. Don't pre-disqualify.

### Identity-based closing
> *Source: [playbook-closing.md](hormozi/playbook-closing.md) — "When They Take The Blame"*

Reframe the buy as identity adoption. "People who become electricians who scale to $1M spend on systems. Are you that person, or are you the one who keeps doing it manually?" New identity → new priorities → easier sale.

### Handling "I need to ask my spouse/partner"
> *Source: [playbook-closing.md](hormozi/playbook-closing.md)*

- "What if they don't give permission?"
  - If "I'd do it anyway" → "Awesome, let's start."
  - If "Then I wouldn't" → "What specifically wouldn't they approve of? Price, program, or me?" → off-ramp and handle.

## MONEY MODEL — the four offer types

> *Source: [100m-money-models.md](hormozi/100m-money-models.md) — "The Four Types of Offers"*

1. **Attraction Offer** — turns strangers into customers.
2. **Upsell Offer** — gets customers to spend more.
3. **Downsell Offer** — gets customers to say yes when they'd have said no.
4. **Continuity Offer** — keeps customers paying.

Great businesses stack multiple. Hughie's path with electricians:
- **Attraction:** Free Missed-Call Audit → paid Speed-to-Lead install ($X setup).
- **Upsell:** Review Reactivation Engine, Quote Follow-Up Bot.
- **Downsell:** Lower-tier "DIY Setup + 30-day support" if they balk at full install.
- **Continuity:** Monthly retainer for hosting, monitoring, copy updates.

Example from the corpus (Micro Gym): Win-Your-Money-Back challenge → payment-plan downsells → supplement-bundle upsells → 12-month lifetime-discount continuity. Six revenue events from one acquired customer.

## RETENTION & LTV — where compounding lives

### 5 Horsemen of Retention
> *Source: [playbook-retention.md](hormozi/playbook-retention.md) — "The 5 Horsemen of Retention"*

Implementing all 5 dropped gym churn from 10–15% → 3% in 3 months. **3% churn = 3.3× LTV vs 9%.** That's a bigger profit lever than any acquisition tactic. Pull the file when designing retention systems.

Heads up: expect churn to *increase* in month 1 ("shaking the three" — billing-zombies get reminded to cancel). Push through — month 2 and 3 it collapses.

### LTGP : CAC (not LTV)
> *Source: [100m-leads.md](hormozi/100m-leads.md) — "Phase Three: Print Money"*

Use **Lifetime Gross Profit** (revenue − cost-to-deliver), not vanity LTV. LTGP is the actual cash you can spend on acquisition, rent, payroll. Healthy ratios: 3:1 minimum, 5:1 great, 10:1+ = print money / scale ads aggressively.

### Lifetime Value Playbook
> *Source: [playbook-ltv.md](hormozi/playbook-ltv.md)*

Tactics to raise LTGP per customer: raise prices, raise frequency, raise duration, raise margin (deliver cheaper), reduce churn. Each is a separate lever — don't conflate.

### HBR-cited fact
A 5% increase in retention = 25–95% increase in profit. Retention is the single highest-ROI place to spend a marginal hour.

## MARKETING MACHINE & PROOF

### Marketing Machine — customers make your ads
> *Source: [playbook-marketing-machine.md](hormozi/playbook-marketing-machine.md)*

Hormozi's gyms: 12 months of top ads, *zero* feature the founder. All customer-generated. "There are way more customers than there are of us — why not use them?" This also de-risks key-man dependency for an eventual sale/exit.

For Hughie: collect a structured testimonial after every install. Build a templated process. Goal: by client #5, ads write themselves.

### Proof Checklist
> *Source: [playbook-proof-checklist.md](hormozi/playbook-proof-checklist.md)*

Setup the testimonial collection process from the Marketing Machine playbook. Fresh testimonials hit inbox weekly. Newest = most credible (no one sorts by "oldest"). Display dates. Show breadth + recency.

### Branding
> *Source: [playbook-branding.md](hormozi/playbook-branding.md)*

A brand is whatever changes someone's behavior toward you because of association. Cattle-brand origin: same cow, different fates depending on whose brand is on it. Branding is downstream of *reputation* — be the brand worth recognizing, not the logo worth designing.

For Hughie: don't waste time on logo iteration. Build the case-study reputation first. Brand = "the AI guy who actually delivered for [Wollongong sparky]."

## FAST CASH — when you need revenue this week

> *Source: [playbook-fast-cash.md](hormozi/playbook-fast-cash.md)*

Mechanic: stack scarcity + urgency + bonuses inside a 7-day window. Daily countdown emails/texts, new bonus per day. For automated checkout, build a first-come-first-serve stampede. For consults, immediate booking but all calls inside 7 days. Notify on milestones: "few sold," "2 left," "sold out," "spot opened."

Use when: launching a new offer, end-of-quarter cash crunch, validating willingness-to-pay quickly.

## MINDSET, DISCIPLINE, LIFE

### Pay the price for impact
> *Source: [100m-leads.md](hormozi/100m-leads.md) — "How Building An Audience Works"*

> "If getting weird messages and hate from people I don't know is the price I have to pay to make the impact I want to have, I'd pay that price any day of the week."

If you want the outcome, accept the cost in full. Wanting the upside without paying the cost = "being a pansy" (Hormozi's word). Applies to public visibility, sales rejection, cold outreach embarrassment, every form of leverage.

### Identity precedes priority
> *Source: [playbook-closing.md](hormozi/playbook-closing.md) — "We Vote With Our Dollars"*

You don't get new behaviors by trying harder. You get them by adopting a new identity, after which the new priorities become obvious. "People who want to be rich spend money on themselves. They spend on education. They invest in skills." Decide who you are first — actions follow.

For Hughie: stop deciding what to do as "a 22-year-old just out of uni." Decide as "the founder of a Wollongong AI services firm doing $10K/mo." What does *that person* do today? Do that.

### Volume negates luck (mindset version)
You're not unlucky in business. You haven't done enough volume. Before optimizing copy, doubling ad spend, redesigning the funnel — do you actually have *enough at-bats*? Most don't.

### Open To Goal (work version)
Throw out time-based work. Adopt outcome-based work. "I'm done when 5 prospects are booked" beats "I'm done at 5pm" every day. Some days are short. Some days are 18 hours. That's the deal.

### The Rule of 100 (discipline version)
Pick the input that matters most this season. Hit 100/day. No exceptions. The discipline of "100 today no matter what" is the discipline that compounds.

### Tiny changes, huge compounding
> *Source: [playbook-retention.md](hormozi/playbook-retention.md)*

A 5% retention bump = 25–95% profit bump (HBR). A 6% → 3% churn drop = 3.3× LTV. Small numbers, life-changing outputs. Look for percentage levers, not unit levers.

### Be the trashman — practice on the wrong people
> *Source: [playbook-closing.md](hormozi/playbook-closing.md) — "Yellows are Golds"*

Don't pre-qualify away your reps. Practice closing on people you wouldn't have closed anyway. They give you the harder reps that make you better faster. Lay-downs teach nothing.

### The Drug Dealer principle
> *Source: [100m-leads.md](hormozi/100m-leads.md) — "Pro Tip: Be A Drug Dealer"*

Give away the *strongest* hit first, not the diluted one. Free value = the first dose. The next one they pay for. Don't water down the lead magnet to "protect" the paid product — the opposite works.
